Career Approach

My career is built on a simple observation: talent is everywhere, but structured opportunity is not. I’ve consistently moved toward spaces with high potential but low infrastructure, developing skills in strategy, operations, and execution so I can build systems—not just participate in them.

I’m less interested in predefined paths and more focused on creating platforms that organize fragmented ecosystems into scalable, high-value industries.

Why Hili Exists

Across Africa, the creator economy is growing rapidly, driven by culture, influence, and digital reach. But behind that growth is a clear gap: creators lack structured systems to monetize, scale, and operate sustainably.

The ecosystem is fragmented—talent is abundant, but commercial infrastructure is not. That gap is where Hili operates.

Hili

Hili is a Nairobi-based content production and creator studio built to provide structured commercial infrastructure around African talent.

We don’t just produce content—we build systems that allow creators to function like businesses, connect more effectively with brands, and convert cultural relevance into long-term economic value.

Why I Co-Founded Hili

Founding Hili was a direct response to the gap between potential and structure.

Instead of waiting for systems to emerge, I chose to build them—creating the kind of infrastructure I believe should exist for African talent.

Hili is not just a company I’m part of; it’s a clear execution of my broader vision: turning fragmented, high-potential ecosystems into organized, scalable industries.

Vision

Long-term, I aim to build platforms and institutions that define how emerging markets capture value from culture, media, and talent.

Hili is the first step in that direction—laying the foundation for a more structured, scalable creator economy in Africa.
